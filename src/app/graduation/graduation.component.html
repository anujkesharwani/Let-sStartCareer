Career Options After graduation

Whether you have just graduated or are planning to enroll for a professional course, you may be wondering what the most promising career option is for you. This is a very important question you may be required to consider while deciding your career path. To pick the best career option for you, consider your passions, skills and financial goals, among other factors and choose an option that may be relevant in the future. In this article, we discuss the best career options after graduation and a few tips on how you can kick-start your career following graduation.

Browsing Career Options After Graduation

As job markets grow and technology advances, the number of career options after graduation are also on the rise. With the large amount of career options and job positions available in the market at present, it is normal to feel overwhelmed or confused on what path to pick. Thoroughly going through all your options can give you a better idea about what your responsibilities may be and what experiences you can gain along the way. This way, you can shortlist your options and choose the one that best suits your skills and professional goals.

Take the time to recognise your strengths, hard and soft skills, interests and passions. This can make it easier to pick a career option that may give you better job satisfaction and ensure that you stay motivated and content throughout your career. Going through a long list of career options available in the market at the moment may be overwhelming, but taking the time to give it good thought is absolutely essential.

Popular Career Options After Graduation

These are some popular career options to consider pursuing after graduation:

1. Digital marketing

As more brands and companies go digital, digital marketing has proven to be one of the best career options for individuals after graduation, especially for those who are interested in working online. Almost every organisation, regardless of size, has a digital marketer or digital marketing team working to maintain their online presence. Due to this, there is a large demand for young individuals with innovative ideas on how to market a brand online.

A few high-demand job roles in digital marketing include social media manager, search engine optimisation (SEO) specialist, analytics expert and content manager. You may pursue a post-graduate diploma course in digital marketing, opt for short-term courses, consider gaining various certifications or take up internships to get acquainted with the job's duties. This also helps you gain knowledge about the tools and techniques you may use as a digital marketer to promote a product, service or business on social media platforms.

2. Hotel management

If you are interested in working in the hospitality industry, a career in hotel management may be perfect for you. Hotel management is a seasoned, ever-green industry and provides a fast-growth career path where professionals have the chance to gain promotions to higher designations very quickly. As you progress in your career, you may look after and ensure the smooth functioning of several departments of a hotel or of multiple hotels.

As a hotel management professional, your responsibilities may include supervising the front desk, housekeeping staff, culinary team and other specialised areas. It may also be your responsibility to hire new hotel staff to fill various positions. With the right passion, dedication and impeccable speaking, planning and people skills, it is very easy to secure higher managerial positions in top hotels nationally and internationally.

3. Chartered accountancy

If you are a commerce graduate or are planning to pursue a professional course in the commerce stream, a career in chartered accountancy may be a good option for you. The Institute of Chartered Accountants conducts the CA exam and to become a CA, an aspirant can clear three exams, the Common Proficiency Test (CPT), Integrated Professional Competence Course (IPCC) and the CA exam. You may also undergo training before you appear for these exams to ensure that you score good marks and clear through your initial attempts.

After completing the first section of the IPCC, individuals can apply for an articleship period where they work with a registered CA for a period of two to three years. After the articleship, individuals may appear for the final clearing exam, which involves clearing two sections. It may take up to three years to become a certified CA and it is necessary to possess a minimum work experience of two and a half years. Chartered accountancy is a career option that comes with a prestigious job title and an attractive salary package.

4. Stockbroking

Stockbroking is an exciting career option that is well-suited for graduates who enjoy challenges. 21 is the minimum required age to join a stockbroking firm or company as a broker. It is necessary to have a basic graduate degree and some experience working at a stockbroking firm. Apart from the position of a stockbroker, you may also pursue job roles such as financial planner, financial specialist and market expert in this field.

To ensure a fulfilling career in stockbroking, you may consider pursuing a post-graduate degree in economics, finance or commerce. You may also consider pursuing short-term certification courses from credible agencies or institutions. There are no formal academic courses that are specific to becoming a stockbroker, so it is helpful to have an academic background in commerce, finance, economics or business administration.

5. Interior design

A career in interior design is perfect for anyone who enjoys research, planning and coordinating projects with a team of professionals according to a client's needs and designing aesthetically pleasing spaces. It requires creative thinking and a good sense of colours, lighting and other decorative elements to turn any space into a pleasing and functional area. Designers may work within tight deadlines and budgets to meet a client's requirements.

It may be helpful to pursue an interior design course to gain basic understanding of the software and tools you may work with, and to gather knowledge about the field and the workflows involved. Depending on where you work, you may also require licenses and certifications to use the title of interior designer. Success in this field mainly depends on the popularity of a designer's work. Exposure and networking are key to building a successful career as an interior designer.

6. Civil services

Civil service is a viable career option for those who are passionate about serving their country and its citizens and making changes to the system to ensure better functioning of the country's infrastructure. It offers a challenging yet attractive career path to students who are ambitious, offering a wide variety of job positions within the government. Civil servants may also have better work-life balance because of the various benefits that come with having a government job.

If you choose civil services as your career path, it is essential that you prepare to appear for the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) examination. You can read books and listen to informative lectures, read the newspaper to stay updated on current affairs and visit libraries to be more knowledgeable on topics such as history, politics, civics and geography. Depending on the service that you get into, a career in civil services provides prestige, great salary and benefits, opportunities for foreign travel and several additional benefits.

Tips To Get Your Career Started After Graduation

Here are a few tips to help you pick a career option that best suits your goals:

Meet with your college's career advisor. Most colleges have free resources such as placement cells or a career office where you can avail career counselling if you are unsure about your career path or would like to gain a better understanding of your career options. Depending on the reputation of your college, you may also join an alumni networking program to meet with professionals to discuss your career plans.

Start networking. Networking within the community is a great way to find niche career options. Staying in touch with your college's alumni association, going to professional networking fairs and staying active on job networking sites to give you a better chance at a satisfying career.

Enroll for short-term professional specialisation courses. Before committing to post-graduate or diploma courses in specialised subjects to advance on your career path, consider signing up for six or nine month courses in these subjects. This may allow you to get a better idea whether a certain area is suitable for you to pick as a career option.

Pursue internships. Many jobs in fields such as digital marketing do not necessarily require you to have a professional degree in the field. You may opt to do internships to gain knowledge and experience, and to enhance your skills.

Stay informed about the industry. It is important to stay up-to-date on what is happening in different professional fields when you are in the process of choosing a career path. You can read professional publications, join social media forums and follow journals of industry brands to keep yourself updated.
